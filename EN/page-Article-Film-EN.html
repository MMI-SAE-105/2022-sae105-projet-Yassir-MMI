Les Yeux dans les Bleus

Here we present the famous documentary that takes us behind the scenes of this emotional competition

This documentary follows the collective life of the players of the French football team and the staff who supported them during the 1998 World Cup. The director shared the life of the French team throughout the match, which he filmed with a small camera.


A rather unusual documentary

Stéphane Meunier's documentary, commissioned by Canal+, of a fluvial duration of 2h37, sets the scene for the reportage in a way, immersing itself almost entirely in the intimacy of the lodge, thus presenting the viewer with a sort of quasi-invincible form of the inverted ornament in France. Les Yeux dans les Bleus uses its length to slowly instil and reflect the atmosphere of a group living in a vacuum. This secret life, widely commented on by everyone without their knowledge, is displayed in a spontaneous and almost lively way, even prefiguring hidden cameras because it does not seem filtered (Dugley complains about his Guininos Puppet, Jiwack was in agony during physio).


A fully transparent film

Broadcast on Canal+ on 14 July 1998, Les Yeux dans les Bleus looks back on several months of national competition, especially since it was played during the World Cup of the same year, with around 23 players and a much-criticised coach. This epic is unparalleled to date. Les Yeux dans les Bleus remains a unique film and offers an unparalleled intimacy on these players, its "sequel" appears to be nothing more than a great communication stunt that allows the "original" to be reinterpreted.

Ouadghiri,
27/09/22 in Montbéliard
