The mythical stadium of Paris Saint Germain

This French stadium is one of the most prestigious stadiums on an international scale. It is the home stadium of the famous PSG Club

The Parc des Princes is one of the most emblematic places in the history of football in France. It is currently the official home stadium of the famous club Paris Saint Germain, it opened as a velodrome and was later given its shape in 1972. Today, the stadium has been completely renovated from top to bottom and is now functional and 100% connected.


The stadium is located in the south-west of the city of Paris, its construction began on 8 July 1967 and it opened on 25 May 1972. It also represents a monument for some as it is one of the largest stadiums in the country. The club now has the right tools to support its sporting ambitions. The Parc des Princes has never been so popular, and today it lives up to the prestige of the City of Light.


The most emblematic stadium in France ?

With over 35,000 season ticket holders, the Parc des Princes is a stadium clearly dedicated to football. The Paris Saint-Germain brand and logo have been etched into the stadium's décor since Red Star's first official match on 10 November 1973. The following summer, after joining the First Division, the young Paris Saint-Germain, founded four years ago, became the resident club of this historic Parisian sports venue, inaugurated in 1897 as a velodrome. Much later, in 1972, the hull will take on its current form designed by the architect Roger Taillibert. The park offers a new generation of natural and heated lawns woven from synthetic fibres that provide faster, more even and more durable surfaces.
